What to Expect

Think of a VetPronto visit like a visit to your own GP - we can help with anything from standard check-ups to skin irritations and allergies, geriatric issues, and your puppy’s first set of vaccines.

Review previous records

Our team will obtain medical records from your pet’s previous veterinary clinic(s) so that your veterinarian can review their complete medical history and arrive prepared for your appointment.

At the appointment

Your veterinarian can perform the examination in any area of the house you prefer - we find that pets are often most comfortable on the living room floor or coffee table. You may wish to provide them with their favorite blanket or toy to make them feel even more relaxed during the exam. Your veterinarian will sometimes be accompanied by a veterinary technician to assist them with handling or blood tests. They will take as long as you need at the appointment, performing a comprehensive physical exam, devising a treatment plan with you, and answering any questions. Appointments tend to be longer than in a clinic - averaging about 30 minutes per patient. After the appointment, you will receive a summary email outlining everything that was discussed at the appointment and any plans for next steps. Should you need to book a recheck appointment, you can request the same veterinarian as desired. Our staff are also available around the clock to answer any questions that may arise after the appointment.
